Who is he?

Linus Torvalds (December 28, 1969, Helsinki, Finland.) is the creator of Linux, the free operating system that runs millions of servers, phones, and computers.In 1991, as a 21-year-old student, he wrote the first version of Linux on his PC. He shared it online for free. He also created GIT (the tool almost every developer uses to versioning software).

Awards

Millennium Technology Prize, IEEE Pioneer Award, Internet Hall of Fame.

Fun fact

He named the Linux mascot Tux the penguin. Loves scuba diving. Speaks with zero filter.
